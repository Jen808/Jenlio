Objectives

It is widely acknowledged that freshly foraged foods are synonymous with quality and freshness. However, their accessibility remains a challenge. Supermarkets serve as a bridge in the food supply chain, but the prospect of directly sourcing from farms offers an appealing alternative.

In this initiative, I conducted research to gauge consumer interest in purchasing food directly from producers. The study aimed to understand the demand for such a method, the willingness of consumers to engage with it, and the feasibility of implementing a clear and efficient system for direct farm-to-consumer transactions.
